Understanding How Narratives Shape Support for a Sustainable Welfare State

NARWEL investigates the cultural narratives that shape public support—and polarization—around policies promoting a sustainable welfare state in Spain. We map narratives across citizens, media, and political elites; profile who endorses them; and test narrative-based interventions to build support and reduce polarization.

Project Context: Political Polarization around Welfare and Sustainability

This section illustrates the sociopolitical landscape that motivates the NARWEL project. Polarization has increased over the last few years, shaping how citizens evaluate welfare and sustainability policies. Understanding this context is essential to designing effective interventions that build support for initiatives to address societal challenges, such as poverty, inequality, and sustainability.

This figure highlights how political orientation shapes support for core public policies, revealing clear ideological divides—but also areas of unexpected consensus. While left- and right-leaning groups diverge sharply on taxation, redistribution, and climate concerns, they converge on priorities like reducing child poverty and protecting the national economy. Mapping these points of conflict and agreement is crucial for fostering more constructive policy dialogue in polarized contexts.

About the Project

Spain faces high political polarization that complicates agreement on urgent challenges such as poverty, inequality, and environmental sustainability. Traditional predictors (ideology, partisanship, sociodemographics) do not fully explain why citizens support or oppose specific policies. NARWEL focuses on narratives—culturally shared frames that justify viewpoints and motivate action—to understand and improve support for sustainable welfare policies.

Highlights

  • Polarization-aware design Narrative interventions to foster constructive debate.
  • Mixed methods Qualitative analysis, NLP/LLMs, surveys, and experiments.
  • Open & FAIR Pre-registrations, data documentation, and OSF repositories.

Methodological Framework

Bottom-Up Approach

We begin by inductively mapping narratives from citizens, media texts, and political discourse using qualitative coding, NLP tools, and LLM-assisted annotation. This bottom-up analysis uncovers the naturally occurring stories that people use to make sense of welfare and sustainability policies.

Top-Down Approach

Building on insights from the bottom-up phase, we design surveys and multi-arm experiments to test which narrative frames effectively increase support for sustainable welfare policies and reduce ideological polarization.

Objectives

  1. General: Understand how cultural narratives shape support for sustainable welfare policies and how they can reduce polarization.
  2. O1: Identify narratives used by citizens, media, and elites about sustainable welfare policies.
  3. O2: Profile endorsers of different narratives using sociodemographic and psychosocial variables.
  4. O3: Evaluate narrative interventions to increase support and reduce polarization.

Research Questions

  1. RQ1. What narratives justify support or opposition to policies for a sustainable welfare state?
  2. RQ2. Which sociodemographic, ideological, and psychological profiles endorse supportive or opposing narratives?
  3. RQ3. Can narrative-based interventions increase support and reduce polarization?

Work Packages

WP1 • Mapping Narratives

Year 1–2

Map narratives among citizens (N≈3,000), media (≈20,000 articles), and political elites. Integrate thematic analysis, NLP, LLM-assisted coding, human validation, and narrative network mapping.

  • Lead: UGR team
  • Methods: Qualitative coding, embeddings, LLMs, network analysis
  • Deliverables: Narrative taxonomy, validation report, preprint

WP2 • Profiling Groups

Year 1–3

Design a national survey (N≈2,000) to assess narrative endorsement and psychological mechanisms; analyze longitudinal CIS data to understand change over time.

  • Lead: UGR team
  • Methods: Survey design, multilevel longitudinal models
  • Deliverables: Open dataset, paper on historical trends

WP3 • Narrative Interventions

Year 2–3

Test adapted U.S. interventions (Aid-for-Agency; Positive-Sum), run a multi-arm experiment with context-relevant narratives, and a conjoint experiment on policy features.

  • Lead: UGR team + collaborators
  • Methods: Survey experiments, multi-arm trials, conjoint designs
  • Deliverables: 3 papers, policy toolkit, briefs
